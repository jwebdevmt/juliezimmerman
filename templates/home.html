How I build

Building Systems That Reduce Friction

Technology should make difficult things easier—not by hiding complexity, but by organizing it thoughtfully.

Over the years I've built and maintained WordPress fleets, real estate integrations, automation systems, static site generators, and developer tools. Each project taught the same lesson: the best systems are often the ones people barely notice. They quietly remove obstacles so people can focus on what they actually came to do.

That philosophy now connects everything I build.

FYN explores how communities can form around shared interests without surveillance or centralized platforms.

Adaptive Experiences explores how planning changes when different perspectives are preserved instead of averaged together.

My writing documents the technical problems I've solved, the assumptions software makes, and the architectural decisions that make systems more reliable and easier to maintain.

Different projects.
One philosophy.

Build reliable systems.
Explain them clearly.
Preserve human perspective.
Reduce unnecessary friction.

Because good technology shouldn't ask people to become more like software.
It should become more helpful to people.
