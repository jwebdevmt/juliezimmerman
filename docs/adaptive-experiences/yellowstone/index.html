Yellowstone Adaptive Experiences

Coming next

The same framework. A completely different kind of uncertainty.

Yellowstone replaces attraction density with wildlife behavior, road closures, weather windows, vast driving distances, safety, and the fact that nature does not follow an itinerary.

Why Yellowstone?

Yellowstone is a strong second domain because the planning problem cannot be solved by optimization alone. A traveler may value iconic landmarks, wildlife sightings, photography light, family pacing, shorter drives, or flexibility—and those priorities create materially different trips.

Potential demonstrations

Wildlife photographer

Early starts, long waits, flexible routing, ethical distance, and the willingness to abandon the schedule when the animals appear.

First-time visitor

Iconic features, balanced driving, realistic stops, and enough margin to absorb wildlife traffic.

Family road trip

Restrooms, food, attention spans, Junior Ranger priorities, safety, and manageable transitions.

Slow scenic experience

Fewer destinations, more observation, photography, quiet, and time to stay when a place feels worth staying.

What will stay consistent

The request will be structured once. Each perspective will respond independently. The differences will be displayed rather than erased. Julie will evaluate the trade-offs and publish the final synthesis.

Status

Yellowstone is currently a framework preview, not a completed demonstration. It establishes the second domain and shows that Adaptive Experiences is designed to extend beyond Disney.
